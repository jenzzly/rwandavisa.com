### Where it happens

All gorilla trekking in Rwanda takes place in Volcanoes National Park, near the town of Musanze in the country's northwest, along the base of the Virunga volcanic range. The park protects several habituated gorilla families that trekking groups visit daily, each accompanied by park rangers and trackers.

### Physical preparation

Treks range from roughly two to six hours round trip, over volcanic terrain that is often muddy, uneven, and at altitude. You don't need to be an athlete, but a reasonable baseline of fitness makes the day far more enjoyable. Regular walking or hiking in the weeks before your trip, plus comfort on uneven ground, is the best preparation.

### What to pack

- Sturdy, broken-in hiking boots with ankle support
- Waterproof jacket and trousers — the forest is often wet regardless of season
- Gardening-style gloves for gripping stinging nettles and vegetation
- Long sleeves and trousers tucked into boots or gaiters
- A daypack, water, and energy snacks

### On the day

Briefings begin early at park headquarters, where you're assigned to a specific gorilla family based on fitness level and interest. A guide and trackers then lead your small group — typically no more than eight visitors per family — through the forest until the gorillas are located. Once found, you're permitted one hour of observation at a respectful distance, which is where the real memory-making happens.

### Trekking etiquette

Keep your voice low, move slowly, and follow your guide's instructions on distance at all times — this protects both you and a critically endangered species. Anyone showing cold or flu symptoms may be asked to sit the trek out, since gorillas are highly susceptible to human respiratory illness.

<div class="info-box"><strong>Best season:</strong> The two dry periods, roughly June to September and December to February, offer firmer trails and are the most popular months to trek. Permits and lodges book out early in this window, so reserve as far ahead as you can.</div>
