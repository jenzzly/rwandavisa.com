### Who needs a visa

Rwanda operates an open-border policy for African Union citizens, who can enter visa-free. Citizens of many other countries can apply for an e-visa online, and a number of nationalities can also purchase a visa on arrival at Kigali International Airport or a land border. Because policy varies by nationality and does change, always check your specific requirement on the official [Irembo portal](https://irembo.gov.rw/) before booking flights.

### What you'll need to apply

- A passport valid for at least six months beyond your travel dates, with a blank page
- A passport-style digital photo
- Proof of onward travel (a return or connecting flight)
- Accommodation details for your stay
- A yellow fever vaccination certificate if arriving from a country with risk of transmission

### How the e-visa process works

The single-entry tourist e-visa is applied for entirely online. You fill in your passport and travel details, upload the required documents, and pay the fee by card. Processing is usually completed within a few business days, so applying at least one to two weeks before departure gives you a comfortable buffer. Once approved, you'll receive an approval notice by email — print it or save it offline, since border staff may ask to see it alongside your passport.

### The East Africa Tourist Visa

If your trip also includes Kenya or Uganda, the East Africa Tourist Visa allows travel between all three countries on a single visa within a 90-day window, which can work out cheaper and more convenient than separate visas — provided Rwanda is one of your entry points.
