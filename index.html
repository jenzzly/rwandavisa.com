Rwanda Visa FAQ - Complete Requirements & Information

Get answers to the most frequently asked questions about Rwanda visa applications, requirements, fees, and processing times.

What are the basic Rwanda visa requirements?

Required documents:

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Completed visa application form
  • 1 colored passport photo with white background (facial features must be visible, no hats)
  • Supporting documents: flight itinerary, hotel reservation, invitation letter
  • Tour agency or travel itinerary (if applicable)
  • Money order or cashier's check for visa fee (payable to Embassy of Rwanda)
  • Prepaid, traceable return envelope (FEDEX recommended)

Note: Embassy does not accept cash or personal checks. Non-traceable envelopes will not be accepted.

What are the Rwanda visa types and fees?

Visa Types & Fees:

  • Holiday/Tourist Visa (V1) & Family Visit Visa (V2): 30 days single-entry ($50), 90 days multiple-entry ($70)
  • Diplomatic Visa (V3-1): 30 days single-entry, free of charge (diplomats only)
  • Conference Visa (V5): 30 days single-entry ($50)
  • East African Visa (V11): 90 days multiple-entry ($100)
  • Reciprocity Visa (V12): Up to 10 years, multiple-entry ($160) - US citizens only, through Washington DC Embassy

Note: Group tourism requires V8 visa type. All fees are non-refundable and non-transferable.

What is the East African Visa (EATV) and how to apply?

The East African Visa (V11) allows travel to Rwanda, Kenya, and Uganda with a single visa. It's valid for 90 days with multiple entries and costs $100. For EATV applications, please fill the specific EATV application form. This visa is ideal for travelers planning to visit multiple East African countries.

Can you help with airport pickup and hotels?

Yes. We arrange reliable airport transfers and provide curated hotel options in Kigali and beyond.

What is the Reciprocity Visa for US citizens?

The Reciprocity Visa (V12) is exclusively for US citizens who visit Rwanda frequently. It's valid for up to 10 years with multiple entries, costs $160, and can only be applied for through the Embassy of Rwanda in Washington DC. Holders can stay up to 6 months per visit (renewable) but cannot work in Rwanda.
